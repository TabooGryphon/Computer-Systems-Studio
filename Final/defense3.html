5-2 Defense


The college version of the 5-2 defense is really the 3-4 front, with one outside linebacker swapped for another defensive end. The 5â€“2 is a popular defense at all levels of coaching, in part because it has simple reads, is easy to coach, and allows coaches to concentrate on technique. This defense is very strong against the inside run, espically the triple option. Against the pass and outside runs the 5-2 is less than optimal due to the lack of speed on the field. However, it is very dependent on having players that can fill the nose-guard and defensive tackle positions. These require two gap players of exceptional size and power. Teams of smaller size may have difficulty running this defense due to its read and react nature.
